Emotional Intelligence

What is emotional intelligence?

The ability to recognize and control your own feelings as well as comprehend and empathize with those of others is known as emotional intelligence. David Goleman, a psychologist, popularized this idea by identifying five important characteristics for an individual who has high emotional intelligence: self-awareness, self-regulation, self-motivation, empathic towards other people, as well as strong social skills.

How is EQ different to IQ?

While IQ (Intelligence Quotient) assesses mental prowess, EQ (Emotional Quotient) measures a person's emotional intelligence. While IQ is thought to be more fixed, EQ can be developed via experiences, introspection, and practice.

Why is EQ important?

Given that you'll typically have good connections with others and with yourself, having a high EQ usually means you'll be happier and more content with life. This then has a favorable effect on a variety of aspects of your life, including career, family, and personal development. Additionally, you're more prone to experience low stress, fewer conflicts, and flexibility to change.
